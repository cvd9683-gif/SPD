02 · Context
Context

A community of editorial designers — built around the work, not the org chart.

What SPD is

The Society of Publication Designers has spent decades shaping how editorial design is recognized, celebrated, and taught. The site holds the catalog of that work — competitions, winners, events, resources, membership.

Why redesign

Years of decisions had outpaced the structure underneath them. Visitors arriving with a specific intent — to enter, to join, to find a resource — had trouble finding it.

04 · Insight
Key Insight

The catalog isn't oversized.
It's under-organized.

From the audit: most pages cluster around five recurring types — but each had been built individually. The work isn't to cut content. It's to reduce the number of templates content lives in.

Source — Content audit The reframe that carried the rest of the project

05 · Direction
Focus & Direction

We chose to focus on structure, not surface.

+Kept in scope

  • Reorganize navigation around tasks Top-level by what visitors do, not how SPD is organized.
  • Five recurring page templates Homepage, competition, winner, event, member tier.
  • Membership as a comparison Match layout to the underlying decision.
  • Lateral content connections Winner ↔ competition lives in content, not the menu.
  • A restrained visual system One display family, one body, one accent — SPD red.

−Dropped or deferred

  • Full content rewrite Editorial follow-up handed back to the SPD team.
  • Custom per-competition styling Replaced by the competition template.
  • A new search feature Nav-level fixes close most of the use cases first.
  • A members-only portal Different problem, different requirements — out of scope.
  • An immediate visual rebrand The system extends SPD's identity, doesn't replace it.

09 · Reflection
Reflection · Outcome

What we'd carry forward.

What worked

Reframing nav around tasks unlocked the rest.

Templates, hierarchy, content surfacing — all fell out of one structural decision more easily than expected.

What didn't

Some decisions ran ahead of user evidence.

Interviews were still in progress when several layouts locked. The templates can absorb feedback, but the redesign is more confident than the evidence yet supports.

What's next

Editorial discipline needs its own system.

Strong components only carry a site so far. Content guidelines should land alongside the design system, not after it.

Quantitative outcomes — to be added once the site has been live long enough to measure against the existing baseline.
